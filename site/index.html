Questions people ask.

Is Lexora free?

Yes. Lexora is free while in beta — no card, no ads. Your vocabulary deck lives in your browser and never leaves it.

What makes Lexora different from Duolingo, Anki, Memrise, Quizlet, Babbel, Busuu or Rosetta Stone?

Lexora is the calm atelier where translators and philology students go when streaks, gems and cartoon owls aren’t the point. Upload the vocabulary you actually need, walk every CEFR level layered with real grammar and philology, and replace gamified noise with a quiet daily ritual that deepens craft instead of farming clicks.

Which languages does Lexora support?

English and French at every CEFR level from A1 (Breakthrough) to C2 (Mastery), including a dedicated philology track for translators.

Can I upload my own vocabulary?

Yes — upload a .pdf or .txt file with one term — translation per line. Lexora parses and de-duplicates the entries, then drills them on flashcards.

Does Lexora cover grammar as well as vocabulary?

Every CEFR level includes both lexical themes and grammar topics. C1–C2 add a philology track covering etymology, historical linguistics, sociolinguistics and translation theory.

Is my data private?

Your deck and study history stay in localStorage in your own browser. Nothing is uploaded to a server in the demo.
